About Me

Hi, I'm Matthew Moliassa. Welcome to my personal site. I'm a third-year student at the University of Michigan, studying Computer Engineering. As someone with parallel interests in technology and business, I look forward to a career where I can combine the two in my work. I am attracted to the innovative and open-ended nature of engineering as well as the fast-pace and risk-taking involved in entrepreneurship. Ultimately, I am an open-minded person with a wide variety of interests, and I am always looking for opportunities to learn and grow.

On this site you will find some detailed information about my background and past professional experiences as well as some personal extracurricular work like photography (such as the cover photo). A more concise look at my experience can be seen on my resume (linked above).

Walter E. Lay Automotive Engineering Laboratory

Research Assistant, May–October 2019

Ann Arbor, MI

In this role, I was exposed to the intricacies of engineering research. While managing the setup of a data acquisition system for a military generator research project, I learned about the attention to detail in the setup process of research. Specifically, I worked on developing a functional data acquisition cart for a dual-engine benchmarking setup as well as updating infrastructure of a test cell for the needs of the research assignment. In particular, this position challenged me to find creative solutions to ambiguous technical problems. In light of unexpected problems or needs, I was often challenged to develop reliable solutions quickly and in correspondence with specific constraints.

Canton Township

Geographic Information Systems Intern, Summer 2018

Canton, MI

Working at Canton Township was an experience of quickly learning software skills and employing them in a practical environment. In my time here, I began with a week of ArcMap and ArcGIS software training, then immediately applied these skills in a personal project. My project consisted of updating a geographic database with utility data for Canton's public works sector and digitally mapping features of local infrastructure. Ultimately, my work provided a valuable resource for local workers to use for positioning verification when working remotely across the township.

Ford Motor Company

Supplier Technical Assistance Intern, Summer 2017

Dearborn, MI

Ford was my first exposure to the professional world. As an engineering intern surrounded by people much older and experienced than me, I quickly matured and found new insight in each workday. Over the summer, I completed an individual project that included analyzing and compiling technical information on 70+ suppliers into a data map, providing much-needed organization for STA managers and engineers. The rest of my time was spent assisting with on-site technical supplier evaluation meetings and shadowing engineers.
